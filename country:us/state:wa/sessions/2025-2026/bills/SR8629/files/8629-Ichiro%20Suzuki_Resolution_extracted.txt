ï»¿SENATE RESOLUTION8629BySenators Schoesler, Boehnke, Chapman, Cleveland, Dozier, Gildon, Goehner, Liias, Lovelett, MacEwen, Nobles, Orwall, Robinson, Shewmake, Short, Trudeau, Valdez, Warnick, Wellman, Hasegawa, Braun, Fortunato, Torres, and WagonerWHEREAS, Ichiro Suzuki's passion for baseball was rivaled by few players before his time or since his retirement; andWHEREAS, Ichiro was born October 22nd, 1973, and grew up in a small Japanese town called Toyoyama; andWHEREAS, Ichiro joined his first baseball team at the age of 7, following a daily routine with his father that included throwing 50 pitches, fielding 50 infield balls and 50 outfield balls, and hitting 500 pitches; andWHEREAS, By age 12, Ichiro dedicated himself to pursuing a career in professional baseball, dedicating his life to the sport; andWHEREAS, Ichiro had established himself as the best baseball player in Japan by the year 2000, which led him to sign with the Seattle Mariners before the 2001 season; andWHEREAS, Ichiro began his rookie season with a bang, hitting safely in 39 of his first 41 games, showing great speed on the base paths and in the outfield, and displaying a very powerful throwing arm; andWHEREAS, Ichiro quickly became a fan favorite in Seattle and the Northwest, with fans at Mariner home games chanting his name before his at-bats; andWHEREAS, Ichiro helped lead the Mariners to the best record in Major League Baseball in 2001 with 116 wins and 46 losses, with the 116 wins tying the 1906 Chicago Cubs for most wins by a Major League team in a season; andWHEREAS, Ichiro finished the 2001 season with 242 hits, 56 steals, an American League-leading .350 batting average, the first of his 10 Gold Glove Awards and three Silver Slugger Awards, and was a winner of the American League Most Valuable Player and was the runaway winner of the American League Rookie of the Year Award; andWHEREAS, In 2004, Ichiro broke the MLB record for hits in a single season with 262 hits; andWHEREAS, Ichiro was traded by the Mariners to the New York Yankees during the 2012 season, and he later played for the Miami Marlins from 2015 to 2017 before returning to Seattle and ending his MLB career with the Mariners in 2018 and 2019; andWHEREAS, Ichiro had the highest batting average of any MLB player who debuted after year 2000; andWHEREAS, Ichiro is one of only seven players in history with at least 3,000 hits and 500 stolen bases; andWHEREAS, Ichiro was the first Japanese position player to sign with a Major League club, and the third Japanese player to sign with the Mariners; andWHEREAS, He became the 30th player to reach 3,000 hits, reaching the milestone on August 7th, 2016; andWHEREAS, Ichiro Suzuki was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame on January 21st, 2025, receiving 393 of a possible 394 votes of the Baseball Writers' Association of America, thus being part of the 2025 Hall of Fame class along with Dick Allen, Dave Parker, CC Sabathia and Billy Wagner;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Senate honor and congratulate Ichiro Suzuki on being elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame and thank him for an incredible career with the Seattle Mariners that earned him the respect and admiration of Mariner fans and other baseball fans.I, Sarah Bannister, Secretary of the Senate,do hereby certify that this is a true andcorrect copy of Senate Resolution 8629,adopted by the SenateMarch 10, 2025SARAH BANNISTERSecretary of the Senate
