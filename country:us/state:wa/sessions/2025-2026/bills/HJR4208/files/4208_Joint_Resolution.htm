HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 4208
State of Washington
69th Legislature
2025 Regular Session
ByRepresentatives Chase, Marshall, Klicker, Abell, Engell, and Dufault
Read first time 04/24/25.Referred to Committee on State Government & Tribal Relations.
BE IT RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE AND HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF WASHINGTON, IN LEGISLATIVE SESSION ASSEMBLED:
THAT, At the next general election to be held in this state the secretary of state shall submit to the qualified voters of the state for their approval and ratification, or rejection, an amendment to the Constitution of the state of Washington by adding a new article to read as follows:
ARTICLE . . .
AUTONOMOUS REGIONS OF WASHINGTON
Article . . ., section 1. The state of Washington shall be divided into two regions: The Puget Sound region and the Columbia region. The Puget Sound region shall consist of the counties of Clallam, Grays Harbor, Island, Jefferson, King, Kitsap, Mason, Pierce, San Juan, Skagit, Snohomish, Thurston, and Whatcom. The regional capital of the Puget Sound region shall be located within Thurston county, unless the Puget Sound region selects a different location. The Columbia region shall consist of the counties of Adams, Asotin, Benton, Chelan, Clark, Columbia, Cowlitz, Douglas, Ferry, Franklin, Garfield, Grant, Kittitas, Klickitat, Lewis, Lincoln, Okanagan, Pacific, Pend Oreille, Skamania, Spokane, Stevens, Wahkiakum, Walla Walla, Whitman, and Yakima. The capital of the Columbia region shall be located within Grant county, unless the Columbia region selects a different location. The Puget Sound region and the Columbia region shall each draft and adopt a regional constitution.
Article . . ., section 2. The legislative authority of the Puget Sound region shall be vested in a regional legislature, consisting of a regional senate and a regional house of representatives. The legislative authority of the Columbia region shall be vested in a regional legislature, consisting of a regional senate and a regional house of representatives. No person shall be eligible to the Puget Sound region legislature or the Columbia region legislature who shall not be a citizen of the United States and a qualified voter in the district for which the person is chosen.
Article . . ., section 3. The executive power of the Puget Sound region shall be vested in a regional governor. The executive power of the Columbia region shall be vested in a regional governor.
Article . . ., section 4. Upon adoption of this article, a judge of the supreme court or a judge of the superior court shall continue to serve the remainder of their term pending their next election.
Article . . ., section 5. Upon adoption of this article, an elected county or local official shall continue to serve the remainder of their term pending their next election.
Article . . ., section 6. Upon adoption of this article, an elected federal official shall continue to serve the remainder of their term pending their next election.
Article . . ., section 7. The Puget Sound region shall own all assets located within the Puget Sound region. The Columbia region shall own all assets located within the Columbia region.
Article . . ., section 8. Each region shall assume the liabilities of the state in proportion to the region's population.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That this amendment is a single amendment within the meaning of Article XXIII, section 1 of the state Constitution.
The legislature finds that the changes contained in this amendment constitute a single integrated plan for the creation of two autonomous regions in the state of Washington. If this amendment is held to be separate amendments, this joint resolution is void in its entirety and is of no further force and effect.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the secretary of state shall cause notice of the foregoing constitutional amendment to be published at least four times during the four weeks next preceding the election in every legal newspaper in the state.
