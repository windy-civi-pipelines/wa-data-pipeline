SENATE JOINT MEMORIAL 8009
State of Washington
69th Legislature
2025 Regular Session
BySenator MacEwen
Read first time 01/15/25.Referred to Committee on Transportation.
TO THE WASHINGTON STATE TRANSPORTATION COMMISSION, AND TO THE HONORABLE SECRETARY OF THE WASHINGTON STATE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION, AND TO THE WASHINGTON STATE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION:
We, your Memorialists, the Senate and House of Representatives of the State of Washington, in legislative session assembled, respectfully represent and petition as follows:
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor was born in 1933 in Covington, Louisiana and lived in Kitsap County from 1936 until his death in 2003; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor was a real estate developer and builder in Kitsap County for 50 years and owned numerous properties in Bremerton, Port Orchard, Silverdale, and Poulsbo at the time of his death; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor developed and built the original Eldorado Hills housing development on the west side of Highway 16, earning national recognition in Sunset Magazine; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor was a Washington State Representative, serving two terms from 1968 to 1972; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor was involved in many business groups in Kitsap County, including the Kitsap County Bank Board of Directors, Olympic College Board of Trustees, and member of the Chambers of Commerce in Bremerton, Poulsbo, and Port Orchard; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor graduated from the University of Washington with a degree in business and was a life member of the University of Washington Alumni Association; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor was the former publisher of the Kitsap Journal; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor owned and operated Kitsap Bremerton Charleston Transit, now known as Kitsap Transit; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor was involved in the building community as a longtime member and President of the Kitsap County Home Builder's Association, now known as Kitsap Building Association, and a local Director for the National Association of Home Builders; and
WHEREAS, Joe Mentor regularly donated his time in many groups throughout the Kitsap County community, including as an active member and deacon of the Silverdale Lutheran Church, Vice President of Olympic Peninsula Enterprises, member of NAACP, Boy Scout Master and member of Kitsap County Boy Scouts Board of Directors, life member of Holly Ridge School, member of Bremerton Navy League, and Chairman of the Kitsap County Cancer Drive;
NOW, THEREFORE, Your Memorialists respectfully pray that the Washington State Transportation Commission commence proceedings to designate the State Route Number 3 overpass at the junction of Chico Way NW in Bremerton as the Joe Mentor memorial overpass to honor his years of public service.
BE IT RESOLVED, That copies of this Memorial be immediately transmitted to the Washington State Transportation Commission, the Honorable Secretary of the Washington State Department of Transportation, and the Washington State Department of Transportation.
