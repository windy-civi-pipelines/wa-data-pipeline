ï»¿HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 2025-4623HR 4623ByRepresentative McClintockWHEREAS, It has been the tradition of the Washington State House of Representatives to recognize excellence in every field of endeavor; andWHEREAS, The Columbia River High School Girls Tennis Team displayed tremendous persistence, endurance, and athletic ability, which resulted in a state team title; andWHEREAS, Emma Lungwitz won the 2A singles district title match to advance to the finals and finished the season earning the 2A Greater St. Helens League singles player of the year award in girls tennis; andWHEREAS, Jenny Serebriakova won her match in the singles quarter final to place fourth overall and was selected first team 2A Greater St. Helens League singles in girls tennis; andWHEREAS, Doubles teammates Annie Morgan and Sydney Dreves placed second in the state final and were recognized by the 2A Greater St. Helens League as doubles team of the year; andWHEREAS, Doubles teammates Shaylin Murphy and Annabelle Madrigal ended their seasons by making first team 2A Greater St. Helens League doubles in girls tennis; andWHEREAS, The efforts and rankings of each player on the team in the state tournament ultimately culminated in a state team championship; andWHEREAS, For his achievement in the championship and in his dedication to bettering the abilities of the athletes in his program, Coach Kevin Erickson was awarded coach of the year by the 2A Greater St. Helens League;NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, That the Washington State House of Representatives congratulate the Columbia River Rapids Girls Tennis Team on their excellent performance at the state tennis championship, and congratulate Coach Kevin Erickson, the team's fans, and the community of Vancouver for this accomplishment; andBE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That copies of this resolution be immediately transmitted by the Chief Clerk of the House of Representatives and to the Columbia River High School Girls Tennis Team and Coach Kevin Erickson.
